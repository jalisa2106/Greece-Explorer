Travel Tips for Visiting Greece

Make your trip smooth, safe, and unforgettable by following these essential travel tips — including what to pack, cultural do's and don’ts, and important health and safety advice.

Do’s and Don’ts in Greece ▼

✔️ Do’s:

  • Respect local customs and traditions. Greeks are proud of their heritage — modest dress in churches and monasteries is appreciated.
  • Greet people warmly. A friendly “Kalimera” (Good Morning) goes a long way.
  • Tip moderately. It’s customary to tip around 5–10% in restaurants and cafés.
  • Carry cash. While cards are accepted in most places, small villages and local tavernas often prefer cash.
  • Try the local food. Don’t miss dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, Greek salad, and fresh seafood.
  • Stay hydrated. Summers can be hot — always carry a water bottle.
  • Use sunscreen. UV rays can be strong, especially on the islands.

❌ Don’ts:

  • Don’t flush toilet paper. Many Greek plumbing systems can’t handle it — use the bin provided.
  • Don’t rely only on public transport in remote areas. Rent a car or scooter for island or mainland villages.
  • Don’t show up late. Greeks are relaxed, but business appointments and tours run on time.
  • Don’t disrespect historic sites. Don’t climb on ruins or remove stones/souvenirs from archaeological sites.
  • Don’t overtip. It’s not expected as in the U.S.; small tips are enough.
🎒 Packing List for Greece ▼

Essentials:

  • Passport and copies of important documents
  • Travel insurance details
  • Local currency (Euros) and credit/debit card
  • Power adapter (Greece uses Type C and F, 230V, 50Hz)

Clothing:

  • Lightweight clothes (linen/cotton) for summer
  • Comfortable walking shoes or sandals
  • A light jacket or cardigan (for evenings)
  • Modest outfit (for churches or monasteries)
  • Swimwear, beach towel, flip-flops
  • Sunhat or cap, sunglasses

Toiletries:

  • Sunscreen and lip balm with SPF
  • Personal medications and a basic first-aid kit
  • Insect repellent (especially for island stays)
  • Hand sanitizer and wet wipes

Tech:

  • Phone and charger
  • Power bank
  • Camera or action cam
  • SIM card (or enable roaming/international plan)

Optional but Useful:

  • Reusable water bottle
  • Travel guidebook or language phrasebook
  • Snorkeling gear (for beach lovers)
  • Earplugs (some areas may be noisy at night)
🚑 Health & Safety Tips ▼
  • Emergency number in Greece: ☎️ 112 (works for police, fire, and ambulance)
  • Hospitals and pharmacies are generally reliable, especially in cities and larger towns.
  • Tap water is safe to drink in most places, but bottled water is advised on some islands.
  • Heat precautions: Wear light clothes, stay indoors during midday heat, and drink water often.
  • Pickpockets: Be cautious in crowded areas (like Athens Metro or popular tourist spots).
  • Travel insurance: Always have it — especially for medical emergencies or lost belongings.
  • COVID-19 or health regulations: Check official guidelines or travel advisories before your trip.
